12  Pigeonpea (Arhar)

In the State of Madhya Pradesh, total arrivals of Pigeonpea during 2019 were 155.827 000’ quintals. The highest arrivals of 26.551 000’ quintals were reported in the district of Raisen and the lowest reported arrivals of 0.001 000’ quintals were in the district of Rajgarh. The average price of Pigeonpea in the State of Madhya Pradesh was Rs. 3612.64 per quintals. The maximum price of Rs. 5180.54 per quintal was reported from the district of Jabalpur and the minimum price of Rs. 1872 per quintal was reported from the district of Bhind.

In the State of Madhya Pradesh, total arrivals of Pigeonpea during 2020 were 543.326 000’ quintals. The highest arrivals of 77.562 000’ quintals were reported in the district of Jabalpur and the lowest reported arrivals of 0.065 000’ quintals were in the district of Umaria. The average price of Pigeonpea in the State of Madhya Pradesh was Rs. 4191.38 per quintals. The maximum price of Rs. 5110.36 per quintal was reported from the district of Narmdapuram and the minimum price of Rs. 1582.47 per quintal was reported from the district of Rewa.

In the State of Madhya Pradesh, total arrivals of Pigeonpea during 2021 were 794.109 000’ quintals. The highest arrivals of 120.754 000’ quintals were reported in the district of Jabalpur and the lowest reported arrivals of 0.002 000’ quintals were in the district of Anuppur. The average price of Pigeonpea in the State of Madhya Pradesh was Rs. 5371.95 per quintals. The maximum price of Rs. 6907.07 per quintal was reported from the district of Dhar and the minimum price of Rs. 1550 per quintal was reported from the district of Mandsaur.

In the State of Madhya Pradesh, total production of Pigeonpea during 2019-20 was 274809 tonnes. The highest production of 57351 tonnes were reported in the district of Singrauli and the lowest reported production of 0 tonnes were in the district of Niwari.

In the State of Madhya Pradesh, total production of Pigeonpea during 2020-21 was 286228 tonnes. The highest production of 49432 tonnes were reported in the district of Narsinghpur and the lowest reported production of 1 tonnes were in the district of Niwari.
